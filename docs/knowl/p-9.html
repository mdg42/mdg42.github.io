With the Natural numbers defined, we can think of extending the set to include negative numbers with no decimal or fractional component. Examples include \(-8\text{,}\) \(-759\text{,}\) and \(42\) (also a Natural number). We write the set of all integers as \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) Notice how all the Natural numbers are also integers. We say that the Natural numbers are a subset of the integers, since every Natural number is also an integer.
